Music Interval Trainer

Welcome to MIT

  • On the next page, a series of ten intervals will be played.
  • You may listen to the interval as many times as you'd like by pressing the "Play Interval" button.
  • Please listen to the interval and identify it using the answer buttons provided.
  • The following shorthand is used for interval identification:
    • P1 = Perfect Unison
    • min 2 = Minor Second
    • Maj 2 = Major Second
    • min 3 = Minor Third
    • Maj 3 = Major Third
    • P4 = Perfect Fourth
    • Tri-Tone = Augmented Fourth/Diminished Fifth
    • P5 = Perfect Fifth
    • min 6 = Minor Sixth
    • Maj 6 = Major Sixth
    • min 7 = Minor Seventh
    • Maj 7 = Major Seventh
    • P8 = Perfect Octave
  • Once you select your answer, click the "Next Interval" button to play a new interval.
  • Once you have gone through all ten intervals, please go on to the results page to see how you did.
